# Why This Website Exists

## Spreading Value  
In the past‚Äîand even now‚ÄîI have always been searching for blogs and websites that share insightful knowledge curated by **skilled craftsmen** in their respective fields. I have learned a great deal from such sources.

Personally, I prefer reading documents over watching videos or clips. When reading, information is often absorbed faster‚Äîand in greater depth.

Of course, videos with visuals and animations can make concepts easier to grasp at first glance. However, based on more than a decade of browsing experience, I can confidently say that a **well-written, credible article**, enriched with images, references, links, and GIFs, is usually far more time-efficient and effective in the long run.

I hope you will find something useful here.

## A Personal Knowledge Library  
Since developing the habit of taking notes, I initially wrote everything down in physical notebooks. Once I had my own computer, I moved to Google Docs. During my professional years, I used tools like Notion and Jira (Atlassian). Over the past three years, I have mainly been using Obsidian.

What I appreciate most about Obsidian is its document management based on `markdown` files. For a programmer, Markdown already provides nearly everything needed to write technical documentation.

All of these tools are excellent‚Äîespecially Obsidian combined with Git, which is free. However, syncing with mobile devices is not always convenient, so I still use Notion and Obsidian in parallel.

That said, Obsidian sometimes feels *too powerful*‚ÄîI cannot fully utilize all of its features. Notion, on the other hand, limits font customization and is not entirely free. To be honest, I am not completely satisfied with how both tools organize and retrieve information.

What I really need is just the **core functionality** of a personal knowledge library:
- Create, delete, and edit notes  
- Filter and summarize content  
- Search efficiently  
- Render basic content formats  
- Support bilingual learning  
- Sync across devices  
- Share one or multiple documents with others  

That‚Äôs it‚Äînothing more.

I want it to be as minimal as possible ü´†. The ultimate purpose of a personal library is **speed**: when I need something, I must be able to find it instantly. Even years later, a quick glance should be enough to understand what the content is about.

That is why I decided to build this website myself. The public version is a shared space, while the private version is a bundled app tailored specifically to my personal workflow, partially synchronized with this site.

## Self-Observation and Self-Improvement  
It has been quite a long time since I have studied and worked entirely on my own.

Because I work independently, there is no one to formally evaluate the quality or effectiveness of my work. When note-taking remains purely private, laziness often overcomes discipline.

By making my thoughts and research public, I am indirectly forcing myself to take responsibility for a concrete commitment. If I fail to follow through, I feel a sense of lost credibility‚Äîand even guilt üôÉ.

It is, admittedly, a rather unconventional learning method.
